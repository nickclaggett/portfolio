1. Problem Statement

Many Duolingo users express frustration online, feeling that they haven’t made meaningful progress on the app or learned practical language skills they can apply in real-life situations.

2. Hypothesis

  • Primary Hypothesis: Introducing a "Vacation Preparation" module focused on travel-specific conversations will significantly improve user confidence and retention rates among casual learners.
  • Supporting Hypotheses:
    • Users will feel more prepared and confident when practicing travel-related conversations.
    • This module could increase overall user engagement and reduce app churn for beginners.
    • Offering this feature as a premium module might increase subscription rates.

Explanation for Hypothesis: User claims most likely stem from limited usage, a strong likelihood to quit, and an inability to recite useful phrases when needed.

This feature aims to assuage these concerns in a multitude of ways

  • Lessons containing common interactions intrigue the average learner more than a grammar-focused approach
  • Fun and relevant lessons that stick in someone’s mind are strong incentives to return and continue using Duolingo
  • When prompted by friends or engaged in a common scenario (ordering coffee) a user can easily bring up their knowledge.

What gap does this feature address?

Current Duolingo lessons focus on broader language skills and long-term learning, but there’s a lack of context-specific modules to prepare users for regular conversations. The gap is between theoretical knowledge and practical, real-world application for beginners/intermediates.

Target Audience: Identify who would benefit from this feature.

  • Target demographic: New language learners who want a crash course in conversation basics for their vacations.
    • Age: 22 - 50
    • Gender: All
    • Location: Canada & United States
    • Income Level: Mid to High earners
  • Focus on B2C users who are casual learners (tourists, business travellers, digital nomads).

Expanded Information:

Babbel:

  • Upon starting a course, users are asked to select their primary motivation for learning. If “vacation” is chosen, they are informed that the course includes over 15 hours of travel-specific French lessons.
  • Babbel Live offers users the opportunity to converse with real teachers on topics of personal interest, allowing for targeted, practical learning.
  • While Babbel does offer a free version, it provides access to only a single lesson per language, functioning more as a trial rather than a fully usable free plan.

Rosetta Stone:

  • Rosetta Stone, like Duolingo, begins its language courses with a strong focus on foundational grammar.
  • Rosetta Stone offers the ability to leave the structured lesson plan, allowing them to explore and engage with courses that match their interests.
  • Rosetta Stone provides live lessons and instructor-led sessions for a more interactive learning experience.
